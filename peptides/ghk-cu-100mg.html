GHK-Cu (100mg)

Format: Lyophilized Powder (Copper tripeptide-1 / glycyl-L-histidyl-L-lysine·Cu2+)

🧬 Mechanism of Action

A naturally occurring copper-binding tripeptide involved in tissue remodeling and repair signaling.

  • Supports collagen / elastin synthesis and extracellular matrix remodeling.
  • Exhibits antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activity via copper-dependent enzyme pathways.
  • Studied for wound healing, skin quality, and hair-follicle support (injectable or topical).

🧪 Reconstitution Guide

  • Diluent: Add 5.0 mL of Bacteriostatic (BAC) Water gently down the side of the vial.
  • Mixing: Do not shake. Swirl gently until fully dissolved. A clear blue solution is normal (copper complex).
  • Final Concentration: 20 mg per 1 mL (200 mcg per syringe unit / 1 mg = 5 units on a U-100 syringe).
  • Storage: Refrigerate at 36°F–46°F (2°C–8°C). Protect from light. Discard if cloudy or precipitate forms.

Optional denser mix: 2.0 mL BAC → 50 mg/mL (1 mg = 2 units). Optional weaker mix: 10.0 mL BAC → 10 mg/mL (1 mg = 10 units).

📅 Protocol & Dosing

Frequency: Subcutaneous injection once daily (or 5–6 days per week). Timing is flexible relative to meals.

Tier Target Dose Syringe Volume (U-100)
Conservative 1.0 mg 5 units
Standard 1.5–2.0 mg 7.5–10 units
Higher research range 2.0–3.0 mg 10–15 units

At 2 mg/day with the 5 mL mix, one 100 mg vial lasts about 50 days.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Blue tint at injection site (temporary), mild site irritation, metallic taste (rare), transient fatigue or flushing.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Copper peptide — do not combine casually with high-dose copper supplements without reason.
  • Discontinue if persistent rash, hives, or systemic allergic signs appear.
  • For laboratory research use only.
