Retatrutide

Format: Lyophilized Powder · Vial 30 mg

🧬 Mechanism of Action

Triple agonist targeting GLP-1, GIP, and Glucagon receptors.

  • Enhances insulin secretion & delays gastric emptying.
  • Suppresses appetite.
  • Upregulates energy expenditure and lipid (fat) metabolism.

📅 Protocol & Titration

Frequency: Subcutaneous injection once weekly (every 7 days). Stay at each step for about 4 doses before increasing, or longer if side effects need more time.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Nausea, diarrhea, vomiting, constipation, decreased appetite, mild increase in resting heart rate, and transient injection site irritation.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Monitor resting heart rate regularly.
  • Discontinue immediately if severe abdominal pain occurs.
  • For laboratory research use only.

💬 Community notes

Anecdotal reports from forums and user communities — not clinical evidence. Experiences vary widely.

  • Often used for: Aggressive fat loss and appetite shutdown; often preferred when tirz/sema plateaus.
  • Watch for: Nausea, food aversion, resting heart-rate bumps, and constipation are the usual early complaints.
  • Avoid stacking with: Other strong GLP-1 / dual agonists at full dose (stacking incretins amplifies GI risk). Be careful with anything that also raises heart rate.
  • Safety vibe: Generally treated like other incretin mimetics: titrate slowly. Not for people with medullary thyroid cancer history / MEN2 discussions, or unexplained severe abdominal pain.
