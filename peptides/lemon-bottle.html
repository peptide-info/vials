Lemon Bottle

Format: Premixed Fat-Dissolving Solution (riboflavin / bromelain / lecithin) · 10 mL vial

🧬 Overview

A cosmetic fat-dissolving ("lipolysis") cocktail injected directly into a localized fat pocket — not a normal Sub-Q metabolic shot. Typical ingredients include riboflavin (B2), bromelain, and lecithin, marketed to break down fat cells in the treated area.

  • Localized fat-dissolving injection for small, stubborn fat pockets.
  • Injected into the target area only — this is not a whole-body Sub-Q protocol.
  • Premixed and ready to use.

📅 Usage & Handling

Guide

  • Local use only: Administered into a specific fat deposit via multiple small deposits — a technique best performed by a trained provider.
  • Ready to use: Premixed — do not add BAC water.
  • Storage: Refrigerate; protect from light. Discard if discolored.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Swelling, redness, bruising, warmth, and tenderness in the treated area for several days.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Not a Sub-Q metabolic shot — improper placement can damage tissue.
  • Avoid over infection, near the eyes, or if allergic to any component (e.g. bromelain/pineapple).
  • For laboratory research use only.
