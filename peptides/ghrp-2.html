GHRP-2

Format: Lyophilized Powder (Growth Hormone Releasing Peptide-2) · Vial 5 mg

🧬 Mechanism of Action

A synthetic ghrelin-mimetic that binds the growth-hormone secretagogue receptor (GHS-R) to trigger a pulse of growth hormone from the pituitary. Frequently paired with a GHRH like CJC-1295 for a stronger, more natural pulse.

  • Stimulates a short GH pulse; usually dosed on an empty stomach.
  • Synergizes with GHRH peptides (e.g. CJC-1295) in research stacks.
  • Can transiently raise cortisol and prolactin more than Ipamorelin.

📅 Protocol & Dosing

Frequency: Often 1–3× daily (e.g. before bed and/or post-workout), away from food. ~100 mcg per pulse saturates the receptor in most protocols.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Head/face flushing, water retention, tingling, and a temporary rise in hunger.
  • May raise cortisol and prolactin at higher doses.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Avoid with active malignancy — GH secretagogues are not used in that setting.
  • Dosing above ~100 mcg per pulse rarely adds GH release but does raise side effects.
  • For laboratory research use only.
