CJC-1295 with DAC

Format: Lyophilized Powder (GHRH analogue with Drug Affinity Complex) · Vial 5 mg

🧬 Mechanism of Action

Long-acting GHRH analogue bound to albumin via DAC, producing a more prolonged GH/IGF-1 elevation than No-DAC Mod GRF.

  • Infrequent dosing (often 1–2× weekly) versus multi-daily No DAC.
  • Less “pulse-like” GH pattern than No DAC + Ipamorelin stacks.
  • Different risk/benefit profile — not interchangeable with No DAC schedules.
Label check: “CJC-1295 with DAC” is not the same as CJC-1295 No DAC / Mod GRF 1-29. With DAC stays active much longer (albumin binding), so weekly dosing and side-effect monitoring differ. If your vial or vendor listing is ambiguous, assume nothing — confirm DAC vs No DAC before mixing.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Water retention, joint stiffness, flushing, fatigue, injection-site reactions.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Do not dose this like No DAC. Multi-daily Mod GRF schedules will overshoot on With DAC.
  • Do not stack with CJC-1295 No DAC or No DAC + Ipamorelin blends.
  • Monitor IGF-1-related side effects carefully with long-acting GH axis agents (edema, joint stiffness, numbness).
  • For laboratory research use only.

💬 Community notes

Anecdotal reports from forums and user communities — not clinical evidence. Experiences vary widely.

  • Often used for: Infrequent GH/IGF-1 elevation when someone wants fewer pins than No DAC stacks.
  • Watch for: More sustained GH bleed → more water retention, joint stiffness, and “always on” IGF-1 sides for some people.
  • Avoid stacking with: CJC No DAC / Mod GRF at the same time. Don’t dose it like a multi-daily pulse peptide.
  • Safety vibe: Community consensus is mixed — many prefer No DAC + Ipa for a cleaner pulse. With DAC needs extra caution and is easy to confuse at pharmacies/vendors.
