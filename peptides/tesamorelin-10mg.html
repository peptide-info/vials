Tesamorelin (10mg)

Format: Lyophilized Powder (GHRH analogue)

🧬 Mechanism of Action

A stabilized analogue of growth hormone–releasing hormone (GHRH 1–44). It binds pituitary GHRH receptors to stimulate endogenous growth hormone (GH) release in a pulsatile pattern.

  • Increases IGF-1 secondary to elevated GH output.
  • Favors reduction of visceral adipose tissue while supporting lean-mass retention.
  • Does not permanently suppress the pituitary feedback loop the way exogenous GH can.

🧪 Reconstitution Guide

Sub-Q

Single-vial reconstitution

  • Single vial: Add 1.0 mL of Bacteriostatic (BAC) Water gently down the side of the vial.
  • Mixing: Do not shake. Swirl gently until the solution is completely clear.
  • Final Concentration: 10 mg per 1 mL (100 mcg per syringe unit / 1 mg = 10 units on a U-100 syringe).
  • Storage: Store reconstituted solution in refrigerator at 36°F–46°F (2°C–8°C). Protect from light.

Sub-Q

Peptide Cart Convenience (3-week load)

For a peptide pen / cartridge workflow, load three 10 mg vials into one 3 mL cartridge so you refill less often.

  1. Reconstitute each of three 10 mg Tesamorelin vials with 1.0 mL BAC water (same as above).
  2. Transfer all three solutions into a sterile 3 mL peptide cartridge / pen reservoir (total 3.0 mL).
  3. You now have 30 mg in 3.0 mL at the same concentration: 10 mg/mL.
  4. Label the cartridge with peptide, concentration, and date. Keep refrigerated when not in use.

At 2.0 mg × 5 dosing days/week, a single 10 mg vial lasts about 1 week. The 30 mg / 3 mL cart lasts about 3 weeks. Calculator defaults on this page use the cart amounts (30 mg + 3 mL).

📅 Protocol & Dosing

Frequency: Subcutaneous injection once daily, typically at bedtime on an empty stomach (or ≥90 minutes after the last meal). Best taken 5 days on, 2 days off to help limit pituitary receptor desensitization (same schedule style as CJC/Ipamorelin protocols).

Cycle length: Plan on roughly a 20-week run, then reassess.

Timing: Night dosing aligns with natural GH pulse architecture. Avoid food/carbs close to injection when possible.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Injection-site redness or itching, transient flushing, joint stiffness, mild edema, tingling in extremities, increased IGF-1–related water retention.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Can raise IGF-1; monitor if running extended cycles.
  • Avoid with active malignancy or uncontrolled diabetes unless under clinical supervision.
  • For laboratory research use only.
