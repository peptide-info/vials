IGF-1 LR3

Format: Lyophilized Powder (Long R3 Insulin-like Growth Factor-1) · 100 / 1000 mcg vials

🧬 Overview

A modified, long-acting version of IGF-1. The "Long R3" changes greatly extend its half-life and reduce binding to inhibitory proteins, so more active IGF-1 reaches receptors that drive cell growth, muscle hypertrophy, and nutrient partitioning.

  • Long-acting IGF-1 analog studied for muscle growth and recovery.
  • Dosed in micrograms (mcg) — potent, so precision matters.
  • Can lower blood sugar; keep fast-acting carbs available in research settings.

📅 Reconstitution & Dosing

Guide

  • Reconstitution: Reconstitute with bacteriostatic water. Example: a 1,000 mcg vial + 1 mL BAC = 100 mcg per 0.1 mL (10 units on a U-100 syringe).
  • Typical range: 20–50 mcg per day is a common research-sheet range, often run in short blocks.
  • Storage: Refrigerate after reconstitution; protect from light.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Hypoglycemia (shakiness, sweating, hunger), injection-site irritation, and water retention.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Growth-factor peptides are avoided with active or prior malignancy.
  • Can cause low blood sugar — do not dose and skip meals; keep carbs on hand.
  • For laboratory research use only.
