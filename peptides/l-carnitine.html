L-Carnitine

Format: Injectable Solution (levocarnitine) · 5000 mg / 10 mL vial (500 mg/mL)

🧬 Overview

An amino-acid derivative that transports long-chain fatty acids into the mitochondria where they are burned for energy. Supplemental L-carnitine is used to support fat metabolism, exercise recovery, and energy.

  • Shuttles fatty acids into mitochondria for energy production.
  • Researched for fat metabolism, endurance, and recovery.
  • Supplied as a ready-to-use 500 mg/mL solution.

📅 Usage & Handling

Guide

  • Ready to use: 500 mg per mL — draw the volume for your dose; do not add BAC water.
  • Typical range: ~200–500 mg per dose, a few times weekly (often pre-workout) Sub-Q or IM. Split larger amounts across sites.
  • Storage: Refrigerate; protect from light.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Injection-site stinging, and a fishy body odor at higher intakes.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Large single Sub-Q volumes can sting — split doses across sites.
  • Consult a clinician if you have thyroid, kidney, or seizure history.
  • For laboratory research use only.
