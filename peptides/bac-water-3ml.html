Bacteriostatic Water (3mL)

Format: Sterile Diluent Fluid (Multiple-Dose Vial)

🧪 Composition & Purpose

Sterile, non-pyrogenic water containing 0.9% Benzyl Alcohol (0.9% added as an antimicrobial preservative.)

  • Designed exclusively for the reconstitution, dilution, and preparation of lyophilized peptide compounds for research use.
  • The benzyl alcohol static agent effectively inhibits the growth of most inherent, accidental bacterial contamination during repeated needle punctures.

🛡️ Handling & Sanitation Protocol

  • Vial Prep: Always wipe the rubber stopper firmly with a fresh 70% Isopropyl Alcohol swab and let it air dry completely before puncturing with a syringe.
  • Pressure Equalization: When drawing volume out, inject an equivalent volume of air into the BAC vial first to maintain equilibrium and prevent a stubborn internal vacuum.

📅 Storage & Expiration Timeline

  • Unopened Storage: Controlled room temperature between 68°F–77°F (20°C–25°C) out of direct sunlight. Do not freeze.
  • Opened Storage: Once the rubber stopper is breached for the first time, store the vial in the refrigerator at 36°F–46°F (2°C–8°C).
⏳ Discard Rule: Must be safely discarded exactly **28 days** after the absolute first puncture, regardless of remaining volume, due to eventual breakdown of the preservative agent.

⚠️ Critical Safety Notes

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Never use for intravenous injection without formal isotonic adjustment; toxic or hemolytic effects can occur from pure hypotonic solutions.
  • Benzyl alcohol can cause localized pain or a mild burning sensation at subcutaneous injection sites; this is a normal characteristic of the preservative.
  • For laboratory research use only.
