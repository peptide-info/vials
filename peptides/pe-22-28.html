Format: Lyophilized Powder (spadin-derived TREK-1 inhibitor) · Vial 10 mg

🧬 Mechanism of Action

A seven–amino-acid peptide derived from spadin that selectively blocks the TREK-1 potassium channel. In rodent models this releases the brake on serotonergic firing and rapidly promotes neurogenesis, giving fast-acting antidepressant-like effects.

  • Selective TREK-1 (KCNK2) antagonist studied for rapid antidepressant activity.
  • Preclinical only — all published dosing comes from mouse studies; no human dose exists.
  • Research shows a biphasic response: very low doses can be neuroprotective, higher doses antidepressant.
