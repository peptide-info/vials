PT-141 (10mg)

Format: Lyophilized Powder (Bremelanotide)

🧬 Mechanism of Action

Synthetic peptide analogue of alpha-MSH. Non-selective melanocortin receptor agonist targeting MC3R and MC4R in the central nervous system.

  • Acts directly on the nervous system rather than the vascular system to modulate physiological responses.
  • Influences dopamine pathways to regulate libido, sexual desire, and erectile function.

🧪 Reconstitution Guide

Pick the route you’ll use — mixes and diluents are different.

Sub-Q

Subcutaneous injection

  • Diluent: Add 3.0 mL of Bacteriostatic (BAC) Water gently down the side of the vial.
  • Mixing: Do not shake. Swirl gently until the solution is completely clear.
  • Final Concentration: ≈3.33 mg per 1 mL (1 mg ≈ 30 units on a standard U-100 syringe).
  • Storage: Store in refrigerator at 36°F–46°F (2°C–8°C). Protected from light.

Nasal

Nasal spray (DIY metered bottle)

Use sterile water for nasal mixes — not BAC (benzyl alcohol burns mucosal tissue). Standard pump ≈ 0.1 mL per spray.

What you need: 10 mg PT-141 vial, sterile water, 10 mL metered nasal spray bottle, sterile syringe, alcohol pads.

  1. Prep: Warm vial to room temp. Alcohol-wipe stoppers and bottle rim.
  2. Reconstitute vial: Draw 2.0 mL sterile water. Inject slowly down the glass wall. Swirl gently — do not shake. Vial concentration: 5 mg/mL.
  3. Build spray bottle: Add 2.0 mL sterile water to the empty spray bottle, then transfer the full 2.0 mL PT-141 solution. Total = 4.0 mL.
  4. Final concentration: 10 mg ÷ 4 mL = 2.5 mg/mL → 250 mcg per spray.
  5. Seal, label, prime: Secure the pump; label peptide / 250 mcg per spray / date; prime into a tissue until mist is consistent.
  6. Storage: Refrigerate the spray. Discard if cloudy, odd-smelling, or after ~30 days.

📅 Protocol & Dosing

General rules: Use PRN (as-needed), roughly 45 minutes to 6 hours before anticipated activity. Maximum 8 doses per month. Do not dose more than once in 24 hours. Start low — nausea is common, especially on first exposures.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Transient nausea (often peaking 30–60 minutes post-injection), facial/upper body flushing, headache, mild nasal congestion, and transient injection site irritation.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Can cause a transient increase in blood pressure and decrease in heart rate after dosing.
  • Do not use concurrently with uncontrolled hypertension or cardiovascular disease.
  • For laboratory research use only.
