Tesamorelin

Format: Lyophilized Powder (GHRH analogue) · Vial 10 mg

🧬 Mechanism of Action

A stabilized analogue of growth hormone–releasing hormone (GHRH 1–44). It binds pituitary GHRH receptors to stimulate endogenous GH release in a pulsatile pattern.

  • Increases IGF-1 secondary to elevated GH output.
  • Favors reduction of visceral adipose tissue while supporting lean-mass retention.
  • Does not permanently suppress the pituitary feedback loop the way exogenous GH can.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Injection-site redness or itching, transient flushing, joint stiffness, mild edema, tingling, IGF-1–related water retention.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Monitor for edema, carpal-tunnel-like symptoms, and elevated IGF-1.
  • Not for use during active malignancy workups without clinical oversight.
  • For laboratory research use only.

💬 Community notes

Anecdotal reports from forums and user communities — not clinical evidence. Experiences vary widely.

  • Often used for: Visceral fat reduction and cleaner GH/IGF-1 support without exogenous GH.
  • Watch for: Water retention, joint stiffness, numb hands, and injection-site reactions.
  • Avoid stacking with: Exogenous GH at the same time for most users; stacking secretagogues + GH is usually unnecessary and side-heavy.
  • Safety vibe: GH-axis compound — malignancy workups and uncontrolled diabetes are common “don’t casually run this” flags in community advice.
