GLOW

Format: Lyophilized 3-peptide blend (GHK-Cu + BPC-157 + TB-500) · Vial 70 mg

🧬 Mechanism of Action

Community “GLOW” repair/skin blend: copper-tripeptide GHK-Cu plus the BPC-157 / TB-500 repair pair in one vial (no KPV — that is KLOW).

  • Typical vial: 70 mg total = 50 mg GHK-Cu + 10 mg BPC-157 + 10 mg TB-500 (5:1:1).
  • GHK-Cu is discussed for collagen / remodeling themes; BPC and TB for soft-tissue repair and cell-migration research.
  • Dose rows use total blend mass. At 2 mL BAC, ~10 units ≈ 3.5 mg total → ~2.5 mg GHK-Cu + ~0.5 mg BPC + ~0.5 mg TB.

⚠️ Safety & Side Effects

  • Common: Injection-site irritation (copper peptides can sting), flushing, transient fatigue or headache.

CRITICAL WARNINGS:

  • Verify GHK:BPC:TB amounts on the vial — not all “GLOW” SKUs match 50/10/10.
  • Same malignancy-caution notes as standalone repair peptides; copper accumulation is a reason to cycle off.
  • Do not confuse with KLOW (adds KPV).
  • For laboratory research use only.

💬 Community notes

Anecdotal reports from forums and user communities — not clinical evidence. Experiences vary widely.

  • Often used for: Skin quality, collagen vibes, and injury recovery in one copper-heavy repair blend.
  • Watch for: GHK-Cu can sting at the site; blue-ish tint under skin is reported. Copper load over long runs.
  • Avoid stacking with: Extra high-dose GHK-Cu on the side without adjusting the blend draw. Indefinite daily use without breaks.
  • Safety vibe: Popular cosmetic/repair blend. Cycle off for copper clearance; verify 50/10/10 labeling.
